About

The work is the spell.

I'm a CTE Marketing and Web Design teacher who treats every classroom hour like a build session. My students don't fill out worksheets. They design campaigns, decode ciphers, debate brand strategy, and ship work they're actually proud of. The wizard voice is real, and it shows up in every assignment, every rubric, every conversation.

I am also a cancer survivor, currently in remission. I continue fighting for those who have lost their battles with this disease. Their fight lives on in me every day, through everything I go through. That perspective doesn't leave the room when I walk into it. It sharpens everything.

Outside the classroom, I write music, build websites, and collaborate with brands I genuinely care about. I've worked with Caddy Collective Culture, built tribute sites for the people I love, and turned a Caesar cipher into a heist game my students still talk about.

You should also know that I golf. Golf is the greatest thing to ever happen to me. I connect the game with so many esoteric principles that you really just have to get out on the course with me to understand. My game has its moments, but I promise I can pull a few good shots when it counts. I'm also starting to build and restore clubs. As of right now I've rebuilt one, the OG Ping Anser 2 from...well, I already said it, Ping. It was my dad's old putter that needed some new life in it. Golf and club building are two hobbies that bring me heavy joy.

Above everything else, I believe in kindness. I do everything I can every day to be kind to those around me. It doesn't matter what anyone looks like, sounds like, believes, or supports. Everyone deserves to see kindness any time I'm around.

Project I

Caddy Collective Culture × Garsen

Marketing Campaign · Real Brand Collab

Collin Jenkins founded Caddy Collective Culture out of Dublin, Ohio and became a genuine friend during one of the hardest chapters of my life. We connected while I was going through chemotherapy. When his brand released the CCC x Garsen putting grip, I knew exactly what to do with it. I reached out, asked if I could use his company for a real classroom campaign project, and he said yes. My students were assigned specific audiences of golfers dealing with conditions like neuropathy, arthritis, and carpal tunnel, and built full marketing campaigns targeting those exact people. Real brand. Real product. Real stakes. The best ones might actually land in Collin's hands.

Project II

The Wizard Vault

Personal Project · Family Tribute

My dad collects coins. Seriously collects them. Gold, silver, graded pieces, full sets. He needed a place to actually track all of it so I built him one. The Wizard Vault is a full coin registry with live spot price tracking, inventory management, condition grading, photo uploads for obverse and reverse, and a complete sales history with profit and loss calculations. He adds a coin, the vault does the math.

Project V

Signal Lost

Sci-Fi Classroom Game · Multi-Stage

A sci-fi classroom game set aboard the ISS Meridian, lost in the Nebulon Expanse. Four crew groups each hold a piece of the puzzle. They have 45 minutes to share intel, decode coordinates, and bring the ship home. Built entirely from scratch.

Stage Access Codes
  • Group 1 · Astrocartographers: DELTA1 · holds the star map fragment, X coordinate only
  • Group 2 · Engine Technicians: SIGMA2 · keeps the ship running, critical systems data
  • Group 3 · Xenobiology Team: OMEGA3 · holds Y coordinate, must share with Group 1
  • Group 4 · Comms Officers: VEGA4 · controls all crew communication channels
  • Navigator View: NAV99 · 45 min countdown begins, coordinates must be submitted here
